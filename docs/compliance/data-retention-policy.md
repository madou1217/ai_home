# Data Retention Policy

- Owner: Security and Compliance
- Last updated: 2026-03-01
- Effective date: 2026-03-01

## 1. Purpose

This policy defines how long operational and customer-related data is retained, where it is stored, and how it is deleted.
The objective is to minimize risk, satisfy compliance obligations, and keep only data needed for product operation, support, and security response.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:
- CLI runtime telemetry and diagnostics
- Remote runtime/session metadata
- Account/profile metadata managed by `aih`
- Audit and security logs
- Support and incident artifacts generated by operations

Out of scope:
- Data owned by external providers outside the platform boundary
- Developer local scratch files that are not persisted by product workflows

## 3. Data Classification

Retention is applied by class:
- `critical-security`: auth events, privilege changes, token lifecycle, audit trail
- `operational`: health checks, runtime metrics, control-plane events
- `product-usage`: non-sensitive product interaction and reliability events
- `temporary-artifact`: transient execution output and troubleshooting bundles

## 4. Retention Schedule

| Data category | Examples | Default retention | Storage profile | Deletion method |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Critical security logs | auth events, access grants, token rotation records | 365 days | encrypted, access-controlled | hard delete after retention unless legal hold |
| Audit evidence | release gates, approval records, change evidence | 365 days | encrypted, immutable append preferred | hard delete after retention unless legal hold |
| Operational metrics/logs | healthcheck, runtime status, reconnect/failure counters | 90 days | encrypted, scoped access | rolling delete via TTL job |
| Product usage telemetry | feature usage, workflow completion counters | 180 days | encrypted, scoped analytics access | rolling delete via TTL job |
| Session runtime metadata | session id, lifecycle status, timing | 30 days | encrypted, least-privilege access | rolling delete via TTL job |
| Temporary artifacts | debug bundles, exported transient traces | 7 days | encrypted temporary storage | scheduled hard delete |

## 5. Deletion And Legal Hold

- All retention windows are maximum limits, not minimum guarantees.
- Automated deletion runs at least once per day for TTL-managed datasets.
- If legal, regulatory, or incident obligations require preservation, Security can place a documented legal hold.
- Legal hold must include scope, reason, approver, and expiration/review date.
- When legal hold expires, deferred deletion must run in the next scheduled cycle.

## 6. Access Control And Least Privilege

- Access is granted by role and business need only.
- Security and SRE have read access to security/operational logs for incident response.
- Product/analytics roles can access aggregated usage data, not raw security event streams.
- Write/delete privileges are restricted to designated service principals and approved operators.
- Access grants are reviewed at least quarterly.

## 7. Monitoring And Evidence

Minimum controls:
- Daily retention-job execution status and failure alerts
- Monthly spot-check of dataset age boundaries versus policy
- Quarterly access review evidence with approvals and revocations
- Immutable audit trail for policy exceptions and legal hold actions

## 8. Exceptions

- Exceptions require Security + Compliance approval.
- Each exception must define scope, risk, compensating controls, and end date.
- Exceptions are time-bound and must be re-approved before expiration.

## 9. Review Cadence

- Policy is reviewed at least every 6 months, or earlier when architecture/compliance requirements change.
- Policy changes must be documented with owner, rationale, and effective date.
